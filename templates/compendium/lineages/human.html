HUMANS

Player's Handbook

In the reckonings of most worlds, humans are the youngest of the common races, late to arrive on the world scene and short-lived in comparison to dwarves, elves, and dragons. Perhaps it is because of their shorter lives that they strive to achieve as much as they can in the years they are given. Whatever drives them, humans are the innovators, the achievers, and the pioneers of the worlds.

Main Traits

  • Ability Score Increase: Your ability scores each increase by 1.
  • Age: Humans reach adulthood in their late teens and live less than a century.
  • Alignment: Humans tend toward no particular alignment; the best and the worst are found among them.
  • Size: Humans vary widely in height and build, from barely 5 feet to well over 6 feet tall. Your size is Medium.
  • Speed: Your base walking speed is 30 feet.
  • Languages: You can speak, read, and write Common and one extra language of your choice.

Variant

If your campaign uses the optional feat rules from chapter 5 of the Player's Handbook, your Dungeon Master might allow these variant traits, all of which replace the human's Ability Score Increase trait.

  • Ability Score Increase: Two different ability scores of your choice increase by 1.
  • Skills: You gain proficiency in one skill of your choice.
  • Feat: You gain one Feat of your choice.

Eberron: Rising from the Last War

Mark of Finding

If your Human has the Mark of Finding, replace all Racial Traits found in the Player's Handbook with these, except for Age, Alignment, Size, and Speed.

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Wisdom score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.
  • Darkvision: You can see in dim light within 60 feet as if it were bright light, and in darkness as if it were dim light.
  • Hunter's Intuition: Roll a d4 and add it to Wisdom (Perception) or Wisdom (Survival) checks.
  • Finder's Magic: You can cast the Hunter's Mark spell and, starting at 3rd level, the Locate Object spell.

Mark of Handling

If your human character has the Mark of Handling, the following traits replace the human's Ability Score Increase trait found in the Player's Handbook.

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Wisdom score increases by 2 , and one other Ability Score of your choice increases by 1.
  • Wild Intuition: Roll a d4 and add it to Wisdom (Animal Handling) or Intelligence (Nature) checks.
  • Primal Connection: You can cast the Animal Friendship and Speak With Animals spells without material components.

Mark of Making

If your human character has the Mark of Making, the following traits replace the human's Ability Score Increase trait found in the Player's Handbook.

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Intelligence score increases by 2, and one other Ability Score of your choice increases by 1.
  • Artisan's Intuition: Roll a d4 and add it to Intelligence (Arcana) or checks involving Artisan's Tools.
  • Artisan's Gift: You gain proficiency in one type of Artisan's Tools of your choice.

Mark of Passage

If your human character has the Mark of Passage, the following traits replace the human's Ability Score Increase trait found in the Player's Handbook.

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Dexterity score increases by 2, and one other Ability Score of your choice increases by 1.
  • Courier's Speed: You gain a base walking speed of 35 feet.
  • Intuitive Motion: Roll a d4 and add it to Dexterity (Acrobatics) or checks involving land vehicles.

Mark of Sentinel

If your human character has the Mark of Sentinel, the following traits replace the human's Ability Score Increase trait found in the Player's Handbook.

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Constitution score increases by 2, and your Wisdom score increases by 1.
  • Sentinel's Intuition: Roll a d4 and add it to Wisdom (Insight) or Wisdom (Perception) checks.
  • Guardian's Shield: You can cast the Shield spell with this trait.
  • Vigilant Guardian: You can swap places with a creature within 5 feet of you when it is hit by an attack.

Plane Shift: Dominaria

Keldon

The people of Keld are human, but they are like no other humans anywhere. They are massively muscled and have gray skin, ranging from an ashy gray-white to a deeper blue-gray.

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Strength score increases by 2, and your Constitution score increases by 1.
  • Natural Athlete: You have proficiency in the Athletics skill.
  • Keldon Resilience: You have proficiency in Strength saving throws.
  • Icehaven Born: You are naturally adapted to cold climates.

Plane Shift: Innistrad

Innistrad Humans

Human characters on Innistrad have the following traits. If you use these human traits, do not use the variant human traits presented in the Player's Handbook.

  • Provincial Origin: Choose one of the four provinces of Innistrad: Gavony, Kessig, Nephalia, or Stensia.

Gavony

  • Ability Score Increase: Your ability scores each increase by 1.

Kessig

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Dexterity and Wisdom scores each increase by 1.
  • Forest Folk: You have proficiency in the Survival skill.
  • Fleet of Foot: Your base walking speed is 40 feet.
  • Sure-Footed: Difficult terrain doesn’t cost you extra movement when you Dash.
  • Spring Attack: You don’t provoke opportunity attacks from a creature you attack in melee.

Nephalia

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Intelligence and Charisma scores each increase by 1.
  • Breadth of Knowledge: You gain proficiency in any combination of four skills or tools.

Stensia

  • Ability Score Increase: Your Strength and Constitution scores each increase by 1.
  • Daunting: You have proficiency in the Intimidation skill.
  • Tough: Your hit point maximum increases by 2, and it increases by 2 every time you gain a level.
