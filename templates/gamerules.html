RULES OF THE GAME

CHOOSE A RACE:

    Select a race (e.g., Human, Elf, Dwarf) which provides specific racial traits like bonuses to ability scores, special skills, or abilities (e.g., darkvision, resistance to certain damage types).

CHOOSE A CLASS:

    Roll 4d6 (drop the lowest die) for each ability score: Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, and Charisma. Alternatively, use the point-buy or standard array system to assign scores.

DETERMINE ABILITY SCORES:

    Pick a class (e.g., Fighter, Wizard, Rogue) which defines your character’s role in combat and adventuring. Each class comes with specific hit points, proficiencies, and abilities (like spellcasting or fighting styles).

    Your ability scores determine bonuses or penalties (known as modifiers) applied to your actions.

PICK A BACKGROUND:

    Choose a background (e.g., Acolyte, Criminal, Noble) which gives your character additional skills, tool proficiencies, languages, and personality traits.

CALCULATE HIT POINTS:

    Your starting hit points are determined by your class. For example, a Fighter has a higher starting hit point pool than a Wizard. HP is calculated using your class’s hit die + your Constitution modifier.
